Kirstenbosch botanical garden

To start the ascent, we started from the botanical garden of Kirstenbosch. This garden is reputed to be one of the most beautiful botanical gardens in the world.
It's important to say that I've visited this park in summer so, if you have to do that in summer to, make sure that you have enought whater and a hat.

Located on the eastern side of the Table Montain, this 528 hectare garden was created in 1913 by Professor Pearson.
Buried in the garden at his death in 1916, his epitaph is "if you are looking for his monument, look around you".
When I've visited this garden at the morning the weather was cloudy but the weather in South Africa is very uncertain and the rest of the day was very sunny.
We say that in South Africa, we can have the forth seasons in one day !
After walking during one hour in between the beautiful flowers and threes of the park, I've found a place where a bridge overlooks the rest of the park.
This cursive bridge is realy atractive and some peapole came to take a picture in this beautifull place.

The skeletton george

After visiting the garden, we went to the george skeletton gorge to start the hike.
This gorge are situated to the back of the garden and it's possible to access whitout pay for the garden.
The george are a little bit diffucult to access for the begeners hikers but they are shady so that's perfect for the hot days.
Unfortunately you will be forced to walk under the sun after the skeletton george, that's will also give you a beautiful view on the city bowl.

During the hike, we were able to discover a magnificent view of the whole city of Cape Town and its surroundings.
This ascencion will take you few hours and you will discover step by step the overview of the city bowl before getting to the top of the mountain.

The top of Table Mountain

We then arrived at the top of the mountain, nicknamed Maclear's beacon, which offers a 360 ° view of the entire Cape Peninsula.
This point, the highest of the mountain is situated at 1070m of altitude.

Finally we got to the top of Table mountain and discover the magnificent view overlooking Cape Town.
